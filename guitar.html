Guitar

The guitar is a stringed instrument, commonly played with six strings, that is able to be played in a variety of different genre's and techniques. In the popular music scene, the guitar has had a large impact in society, even as far back in the fifteenth century.

History

During the fifteenth century, the roots of the guitar began to take shape. Its early predecessors, such as the lute and vihuela, laid the groundwork for the instrument we now know as the guitar. These early forms featured various numbers of strings and intricate designs, reflecting the diversity of musical styles emerging during that era.

As time progressed, the guitar underwent several transformations. In the sixteenth century, the guitar gained popularity in Spain, becoming an important part of Spanish culture.

By the nineteenth century, innovations like the use of metal strings and the development of the classical guitar solidified its presence in music. Aswell as the development of folk and country music embraced the guitar, which played a huge role in capturing the essence of storytelling.

However, it was in the twentieth century that the guitar truly came into its own. The advent of the electric guitar in the early 1930s revolutionized music. With the ability to amplify its sound, the electric guitar fueled the birth of rock 'n' roll, blues, and countless other genres, influencing generations of musicians.

Types of Guitar

  • Acoustic Guitar: widely recognized for its rich and melodic sounds, making it an essential instrument in folk, country, and singer-songwriter music styles.

  • Electric Guitar: used with amplified sound and has versatile capabilities that is a staple in rock, blues, and jazz music.

  • Classical Guitar: historically used for its classical repertoire of older music pieces that are linked to this type of guitar.
